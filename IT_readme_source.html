Learning and assessments, and applying this in the ‘real world’ (show/hide)

As a participant in a course, you may have the following questions:

1.     Why is this learning material in the course, it doesn’t relate to what I do?
2.     When doing an assessment, I didn’t learn about this in the learning material. Why am I being asked this?

Learning resources and assessments are provided in your course to meet the Australian Quality Training Framework (AQTF) national set of standards. This framework applies to secondary school, Vocational Education and Training (VET), and to Higher Education studies.

One of the requirements in the AQTF is that the training package which your course relates to must involve industry discussion to identify what the industry requires. From this discussion, a Unit of Competency is developed which contains ‘Elements’ and ‘Performance Criteria’ that provide broad requirements of the skills and knowledge the industry requires. This is assessed against ‘Performance Evidence’ and ‘Knowledge Evidence’ to ensure that a participant can demonstrate the knowledge and skills required.

In your course Upskilled provide a range of learning resources that are selected to help develop the skills and knowledge that the AQTF and the industry requires for the qualification that you are studying. These resources are often provided based on a person having minimal or no knowledge, so it may seem very large and daunting.  However, if you find that you know some of the learning, you can skip it and move onto something else.

The learning resources may relate directly to only one unit of competency, or to several units of competency, or build up knowledge over the course – the second and last options are called clustered learning. With clustered learning, you develop some skills and knowledge immediately and are assessed on them at that point of learning, whereas other skills and knowledge will be developed and assessed throughout the course. Clustered learning has the advantage of allowing participants to build the bigger picture of knowledge and understanding over the course, and reinforces knowledge and learning at several points in the course.

How does this fit in with my written assessments?

You may find that some questions in an assessment do not appear to directly relate to the learning material. The type of question asked will be similar to that you will get in the industry, and require you to connect different pieces of learning together to identify the answer. So, although you may not directly have covered the question, the learning will have been there and you need to connect the pieces.

As a good learning habit, we suggest you review any written assessment instructions and the assessment rubric, before commencing the learning material. This will then set your mind to focus on the knowledge that is required in the assessment.

Learning resources and assessments are provided to enable participants to have a qualification that meets both the AQTF standards, and addresses industry needs.

Assessments are designed to test you on the learning materials that have preceded it.

Quizzes are timed and contain different types of questions like, multichoice, choose one or many, short answer, fill in the blank, etc.

Assignments and Projects will have an instructions document preceding it and if supporting information is required, it will be in documentation listed after it.

Assignments and Projects will also have a marking guide or rubric for you to see what the assessor is looking for in your submission.

Grades (show/hide)

In the settings block on the left of the screen you will see a 'grades' link. This will take you to your grades for all of your assessments. Use this to check your progress and review any comments left by your teacher.

- = This assessment has not yet been attempted or submitted or graded.

NR = Not Required - Not often used, but you may see this from time to time, it is administrative and you can ignore it.

RPL = Recognition of Prior Learning - You have been granted a credit based on prior learning you have undertaken.

NYC = Not Yet Competent - You have not met the requirements yet, and will need to review feedback and resubmit.

C = Competent - You have met the requirements. This is your goal!
